      LVM

      First start with LVM (Logical Volume Manager) What is LVM? LVM stands for Logical Volume Manager, a device mapper framework that provides logical volume management for the Linux kernel. LVM allows administrators to create, resize, and delete volumes dynamically, offering more flexibility than traditional partitioning schemes. Why use LVM? Dynamic resizing: Easily resize (extend/reduce) logical volumes without unmounting. Snapshot support: Create point-in-time snapshots for backup or testing. Volume grouping: Aggregate multiple physical devices into a single storage pool. Migration: Move volumes across physical devices live. Flexibility: Logical volumes can span across multiple disks.
